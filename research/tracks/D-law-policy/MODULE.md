# Track D - Law And DOT Convenience

Papers in this track:

- D.0+convenience-of-commerce
- D.1+public-disruption-metrics
- D.2+reform-pathways

## Module Question

How should ZONES translate DOT's convenience-of-commerce standard, DST law, and
local disruption evidence into analytic constraints without overstating legal
authority?

## Hypothesis Gate

Before drafting, each paper must pass a `/researchprewrite`-style check:

- state the legal or policy claim,
- cite primary legal/regulatory sources,
- distinguish current law from proposed legislation,
- define operational proxies for convenience of commerce,
- define what evidence would block recommendation language.
